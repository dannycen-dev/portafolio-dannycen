## AI doesn’t replace judgment

I use **Cursor, Claude Code, and Warp** daily to write, debug, and document faster. What I don’t delegate: architecture, security, and “should this even exist?”

## Practices that work for me

1. **Short, verified context** — real file/error, not novels
2. **Small changes** — reviewable PRs, not monsters
3. **Tests as a net** — Playwright / unit where it hurts
4. **Secrets out of chat** — env vars, never keys in prompts

## Client outcome

Less time on boilerplate, more time integrating systems (CRM, APIs, bots) where business value is obvious.

The useful question isn’t “do we use AI?” — it’s **“who owns the final decision?”**.
